Understanding the Loss of Hair

Before using any hair re-growth tablets, hair re-growth pills or hair re-growth medicine, it is crucial that you understand the causes that lead to hair loss. These are inclusive of environmental factors, hormonal changes, and genetics but are not just confined to these. Here are the details on every aspect:

1. Genetics

Genetics is certainly that occupies for the top spot as being a cause for the loss, however, the whole idea is slightly more complex than anyone could ever think. Most people must have heard that it is the genetic DNA from the mother’s end that contributes to hair growth or hair loss. It is true partially because the X chromosome that is inherited, is inherited from the women and carries the androgenic receptor with it. A mutation or variation in the androgenic receptor has been accounted to cause female pattern baldness and male pattern. However, there are studies indicating that there could be many other epigenetic factors which can be attributed to it.

2. Changes in Hormones

When hormones in the body undergo change, it poses a serious threat to the follicles of hair. This hormone is Dihydrotestosterone that is derived from the male hormone. Studies have even suggested that when testosterone that is the male hormone converts into DHT. This usually happens in the hair follicle’s oil glands. DTH is responsible for binding the follicular receptors of androgen. It results in hair follicle shrinking that creates one of the most unhealthy environments so that there is the occurrence of hair growth. High DHT levels lead to loss of hair ultimately.

3. Factors Related to Environment

With hormonal and genetic factors attribute to the loss of hair, it has to be noted that there is a multitude of multiple factors that influence rates at which hair falls. Excessive radicals that are free flowing in the body are defined as a catalyst for the hair cells that are damaging. These radicals are clusters that are highly unstable molecules that actually steal the electrons from various other molecules. Hence, the structure is mutated or damaged. Free radicals are found in cigarette smoke, radiation, pollution, and herbicides.

4. Life Cycle of Hair Growth

There are three stages of hair follicle growth and that happens naturally through three stages that are distinct. These stages include the telogen stage, catagen and anagen. When you understand things that happen at each of the stages, you shall understand the hair follicle’s natural cycle, allowing one to see where the process can be improved.
If you ever go through the diagram of hair growth, you shall understand the process of hair growth that begins with the phase of anagen. This phase is referred to as the stage of growth and last typically for two to six years. Interestingly there is enough evidence for suggesting that this phase is ethnically variable.
When compared to the catagen phase, the phase of catagen has a shorter life than the anagen phase. This is referred to as the stage of transition. This means that in this stage original hair transitions for making ample room for the follicles of hair. This period lasts for ten days, typically.

Finally and the third stage is called the phase of telogen. It is also called stationary stage and this stage hair gets released from follicles that are original, later falling out. Remaining follicle sits patiently then for three months at the maximum before the final process gets repeated again.

The hair follicles are independent in nature and thus the timing of every hair follicle through the three stages is actually staggering. The aim of the dietary and nutritional formulas has been prolonging telogen phase that is inevitable because the anagen’s phase length is extended. Undoubtedly, the phase period of telogen can also be reduced, that tends to be an amazing benefit. Luckily there are some natural vitamins and ingredients that exist in making both the things happen.
